Hover Effects : 100% Effective?

The Power of Hover Effects in Web Design - 18 April 2023

When you stare at a still image and something suddenly moves, your eyes will automatically be attracted to that movement. This fact is used strategically in design. As well as drawing attention, effects can make a digital interface more immersive & interactive. Now, we are going to look at a specific type of movement used in web design: hover effects. A hover effect takes place when a user hovers over an element, and the element responds with transition effects. Take note, when browsing a web page. Hover your mouse over everything & see which effects you notice. See how you feel about the interaction & how it would feel if the effect wasn’t there. Let's look at some examples:

  • Viewing a music playlist, you hover over a song & it’s row is highlighted.
  • Visiting a museum's digital gallery, you hover over a painting & it gets bigger on your screen.

Now, imagine these scenarios without hover effects. When viewing a list, such as the playlist example, the highlight effect adds functionality for the user. It’s much easier to see an item on a list when the row is highlighted. Effects should be part of the design, not an afterthought. Some other uses for hover effects:

  • Better user feedback
  • Visual appeal
  • Highlight important content
  • Functionality
  • Keep people engaged longer
  • Provide tooltips
  • Creating animations

Each hover effect should serve a specific purpose for the user. They're best used sparingly & intently. Too many effects can be confusing & distracting. Good effects should be seamless. CSS can be utilized to create hover effects. Effect animations can be customized & uniquely made. Here are some examples in action:

  • Highlighted
  • Shadow
  • Fade in
  • Anything Goes

Interactivity is proven to increase traffic & time spent on your website. Think about it, the more a user can interact with your website, the more time they’ll spend on it.
