Hover Effects : 100% Effective?

18 April 2023
Image

Movement draws attention. Movement can make a digital interface more immersive & interactive. Effects & animations are a powerful way to draw attention in web design: specifically, hover effects. Take note, when browsing a web page. Hover your mouse over everything & see which effects are being implemented. See how you feel about the interaction & how it would feel if the effect wasn’t there. For example:

  • You’re viewing a playlist on Spotify. You hover over a song & it’s highlighted for you.
  • You’re visiting an artist’s portfolio. You hover over an image & it gets bigger on your screen.
  • You’re shopping on Amazon. You hover over the star-rating & a window pops up, detailing the star-rating categories.

Now, imagine these scenarios without hover effects. When viewing a list, such as the Spotify playlist example, the highlight effect adds functionality for the user. It’s much easier to see an item on a list that’s highlighted. Effects should be part of the design, not an afterthought. Uses: better user feedback, visual appeal, highlight important content, add functionality, keep people engaged longer, provide tooltips, or creating animations. It’s best used sparingly & intently. Each hover effect should serve a specific purpose for the user. Too many effects can be confusing & distracting. Good effects should be seamless. CSS can be utilized to create hover effects. Effect animations can be customized & uniquely made. Some examples: Highlighted border, shadow, fade in, grow, skew, overlay, zoom, or any combination of effects. (show with hover effects) Interactivity is proven to increase traffic & time spent on your website. Think about it, the more a user can interact with your website, the more time they’ll spend on it.
